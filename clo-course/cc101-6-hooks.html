Hooks: automatic reactions

Hooks are the autopilot layer. A hook is an automatic action that fires at a set moment while your AI Employee works, so the right thing happens on its own and you never have to remember to trigger it. This is the part that makes the whole system dependable.

⚡ What a hook is

What it is. A hook is an automatic action that fires at a set moment. After every save run the check, when a session starts load my context. They run outside the conversation, so they cost zero of your working memory, and you never have to remember to trigger them.

Why you use it. The best move is to build a quality inspector right into a skill. Take a write-email skill with a hook that says "before finishing, review this email. Does it sound like a real person, not AI? If it's robotic, rewrite it." You only ever see the polished version. Every hook is three parts. WHEN is the event, WHAT is the thing it watches, and DO is the action, run a check, ask a yes/no, or spawn a reviewer.

This is also how a harness fires. The automatic check you added to a skill back in the Skills module runs on a hook, usually the Stop event.

Go deeper: the 14 events

There are 14 events in total. Session start and end, before and after a tool runs, before you submit a prompt, when a sub-agent starts or stops, when Claude finishes, and more. You'll mostly meet them through skills, not by wiring them by hand.

The one worth knowing by name is Stop. It fires right before you see the result, and it can send the work back for a rewrite. That's the quality gate, and it's where most harnesses live.
